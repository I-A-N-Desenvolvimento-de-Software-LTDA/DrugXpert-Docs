# Complete Example: Caffeine

To illustrate, caffeine is a complex molecule that includes multiple rings, different types of bonds, and branching.

**SMILES Structure for Caffeine**:
- **SMILES**: **CN1C=NC2=C1C(=O)N(C(=O)N2C)C**

**Explanation**:
1. **"C"** represents a methyl group connected to the nitrogen.
2. **"N1"** marks the start of one ring, while the following **"1"** closes that cycle.
3. **"C=NC2"** represents another part of the ring linking to other atoms.
4. **"(=O)"** indicates double bonds to oxygen in the structure.

This SMILES sequence describes the entire structure of caffeine, including its rings, bonds, and branches.
