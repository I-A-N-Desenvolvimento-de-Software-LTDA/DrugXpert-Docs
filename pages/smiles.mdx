
# SMILES Concepts and Rules

The **SMILES** (Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System) notation encodes molecular structures in a simple text-based format for efficient data storage, transfer, and computation. Below are the key concepts and rules that define SMILES.

## Atoms and Element Symbols
Atoms are represented by their symbols (C, O, N). Carbon can be implicit, while other atoms are explicit. Square brackets `[]` are used for atoms with formal charges or metals.

**Examples**:
- **Methane**: `C`
- **Ammonia**: `N`
- **Chlorine ion**: `[Cl-]`

## Bonds
- **Single Bonds**: Default (no symbol)
- **Double Bonds**: `=`
- **Triple Bonds**: `#`
- **Aromatic Bonds**: Lowercase letters for aromatic rings (e.g., `c1ccccc1` for benzene).

##  Branching
Branches are shown with parentheses `()`.

**Example**:
- **Isopropanol**: `CC(C)O` (branch at the central carbon).

## Cyclic Structures
Cycles are indicated by matching numbers at the start and end of the ring.

**Example**:
- **Cyclohexane**: `C1CCCCC1` (1 starts and ends the ring).

## Charged Atoms
Charges are shown within square brackets, with `+` or `-` for positive/negative charges.

**Examples**:
- **Ammonium ion**: `[NH4+]`
- **Chloride ion**: `[Cl-]`

## Stereochemistry
Chirality is specified with `@` for configuration around chiral centers.

**Example**:
- **Lactic Acid (R or S)**: `C[C@H](O)C(=O)O`.

## Aromaticity
Lowercase symbols represent aromatic atoms.

**Example**:
- **Benzene**: `c1ccccc1`.

## Hydrogens
Hydrogens are implicit unless specified within square brackets.

**Example**:
- **Methane**: `C` (implied hydrogens).

---

**Summary**: SMILES provides a compact way to represent molecules, capturing atom types, bonds, charges, and stereochemistry in a linear format.
